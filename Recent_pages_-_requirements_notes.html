<h1>Recent pages - requirements notes</h1> <p>2023-08-21: these notes from <a class="wikilink" href="/developer-massive-wiki/conversationsWiki/People/Peter_Kaminski.html">Peter Kaminski</a> are copied from CSC Mattermost Massive-Wiki channel chat on the use cases for including extracts from recent pages on the "Recent pages" wiki page.</p> <p>"Abstract" is a generalization. telescoping back from "displays the first 257 characters", what are we trying to help the reader with?</p> <p>Possible answers:</p> <ul> <li>to help remind them what this page is about</li> <li>to help them decide whether they want to read this page</li> <li>to understand better what has happened with this page.</li> </ul> <p>So then, possible implementations of some of those use cases include:</p> <ul> <li>extract the first 257 characters (without headers) (<code>lede</code>)</li> <li>extract the first five words of consecutive paragraphs, until you've accumulated 257 characters</li> <li>construct an extractive or abstractive summary of the whole page that fits into 257 characters</li> <li>don't focus on content, but rather changes; choose some way to pick the most important diff lines that fit into 257 characters</li> <li>etc.</li> </ul> <p>Simplifying all that product management discussion and decisions, I think it's fine to go with "extract the first 257 characters (without headers) (<code>lede</code>)". But, I'd probably call it <code>abstract</code>, because even if the first implementation is the lede, maybe there will want to be more options later.</p>
