<h1>Ontology (back to basics)</h1> <ul> <li><a class="wikilink" href="/developer-massive-wiki/conversationsWiki/People/Peter_Kaminski.html">Peter Kaminski</a>: Some back-to-basics, for my own edification from <a href="https://chat.collectivesensecommons.org/agora/pl/kjd3ngk8opriir3ph51pdtg4xy">CSC Agora</a></li> </ul> <p><strong>Ontology (information science)</strong> - Formal representation of knowledge as a set of concepts within a domain. May or may not be hierarchical.</p> <p><strong>Taxonomy</strong> - A scheme of classification, especially a hierarchical classification, in which things are organized into groups or types.</p> <p>Note that ontology (information science) is not the same as <strong>ontology (philosophy)</strong>, which deals with concepts such as existence, being, becoming, and reality.</p> <p>Also see Tom Gruber's writeups of ontology (information science) from 1992/1993 and 2009:</p> <ul> <li><a href="http://www-ksl.stanford.edu/kst/what-is-an-ontology.html">What is an Ontology?</a> (1992/1993)</li> <li><a href="https://tomgruber.org/writing/definition-of-ontology">Definition of Ontology </a> (2009)</li> </ul> <p>And Kurt Cagle's compare/contrast of taxonomy and ontology:</p> <ul> <li><a href="https://www.forbes.com/sites/cognitiveworld/2019/03/24/taxonomies-vs-ontologies/">Taxonomies vs. Ontologies</a> (2019)</li> </ul> <p>And lastly an older paper, with nothing particularly pretty, but a good survey of many types of ontology visualization, <a href="http://entrezneuron.googlecode.com/svn-history/r2/trunk/references/12-onto-vis-survey-final.pdf">Ontology Visualization Methods â€“ A Survey</a> (PDF) (2007)</p> <p>cf. <a class="wikilink" href="/developer-massive-wiki/conversationsWiki/images/Jacob_2003-OntologiesandtheSemanticWeb.png">images/Jacob_2003-OntologiesandtheSemanticWeb.png</a></p> <p>Related pages:</p> <ul> <li><a class="wikilink" href="/developer-massive-wiki/conversationsWiki/Wikis_and_knowledge_structures.html">Wikis and knowledge structures</a></li> </ul>
